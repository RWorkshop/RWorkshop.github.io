\subsection{Diagnostic Plots} \textbf{\emph{Homoscedascity }}(constant variance) is one of the assumptions required in a regression analysis in order to make valid statistical inferences about population relationships. Homoscedasticity requires that the variance of the residuals are constant for all fitted values, indicated by a uniform scatter or dispersion of data points about the trend line (i.e. ``The Zero Line"). From the above plot, we can conclude that the constant variance assumption is valid.
